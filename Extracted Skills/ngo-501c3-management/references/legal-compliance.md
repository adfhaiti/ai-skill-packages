## Formation Process

### Step 1: Incorporate at the State Level

File Articles of Incorporation with the Secretary of State in the chosen state of
incorporation. Required elements:

- Corporate name (check availability)
- Statement of purpose (must meet IRS 501c3 requirements — charitable, religious,
  educational, scientific, literary, testing for public safety, fostering national or
  international amateur sports competition, preventing cruelty to children or animals)
- Dissolution clause (assets to another 501c3 or government for public purpose)
- Registered agent and incorporator information
- Board of directors (minimum varies by state; typically 1-3 directors)

### Step 2: Obtain EIN

Apply for Employer Identification Number from the IRS using Form SS-4. Available online
immediately at irs.gov. Required even with no employees.

### Step 3: Adopt Bylaws

Draft and adopt organizational bylaws covering: board structure and meetings, officer
roles, committees, conflict of interest policy, indemnification, amendment procedures.

### Step 4: File for Tax-Exempt Status

See Form 1023 / 1023-EZ section below. File within 27 months of incorporation for
retroactive tax-exempt status to the date of incorporation.

### Step 5: State-Level Registrations

After IRS determination: register for state tax exemption, charitable solicitation
registration (if fundraising), payroll tax accounts, and any local business licenses.

## Form 1023 & 1023-EZ

### Form 1023-EZ (Streamlined)

**Eligibility:** Organizations with gross receipts ≤ $50,000 in any of the past 3 years
and projected ≤ $50,000 in the next 3 years, and total assets ≤ $250,000. Not available
for churches, hospitals, schools, or certain other organization types.

- Filing fee: $275 (2024)
- Filed online at pay.gov
- Eligibility worksheet determines qualification

### Form 1023 (Full)

**Required when:** Organization exceeds 1023-EZ thresholds or is ineligible for EZ.
Includes 11 parts covering organizational structure, narrative description of activities,
compensation arrangements, financial data, and governance policies.

- Filing fee: $600 (2024)
- Processing time: 3-12+ months
- Must include: Articles of Incorporation, Bylaws, conflict of interest policy,
  narrative of activities, 3-year financial projections or actual financial data

### Key Application Elements

- **Narrative of activities:** Describe each program in detail. IRS examines whether
  activities further exempt purposes.
- **Compensation:** Disclose compensation of officers, directors, key employees, and
  highest-compensated independent contractors. Demonstrate reasonableness.
- **Governance:** Describe board composition, relationships between board members and
  key employees, and adoption of key policies.

## Public Charity vs. Private Foundation

### Default Classification

Organizations are private foundations unless they demonstrate public charity status under
IRC Section 509(a).

### Public Charity Categories

1. **509(a)(1):** Traditional public charities (churches, schools, hospitals) and
   organizations receiving broad public support
2. **509(a)(2):** Organizations receiving revenue from exempt functions plus public support
3. **509(a)(3):** Supporting organizations to 509(a)(1) or (a)(2) organizations

### Public Support Test (509a1/170b1Avi)

Must receive at least 33⅓% of total support from public sources (individuals, corporations,
other public charities, government grants). Computed over 5-year period including current
year. For 509a2, must receive at least 33⅓% from program service revenue, membership fees,
and gross receipts from exempt functions.

**Facts and circumstances test:** Organizations with 10-33⅓% public support may still
qualify if they demonstrate a continuous and bona fide fundraising program and other
factors showing public character.

### Private Foundation Rules (if applicable)

- Required annual distributions (minimum 5% of net investment assets)
- Prohibition on self-dealing (IRC §4941)
- Excess business holdings limitations (IRC §4943)
- Jeopardizing investments prohibition (IRC §4944)
- Taxable expenditures prohibition (IRC §4945)
- Higher excise tax on net investment income (1.39%)

## IRS Form 990 Series

### Form 990-N (e-Postcard)

**Eligibility:** Gross receipts normally ≤ $50,000. Eight-question electronic filing. Due
15th day of 5th month after fiscal year end. No financial data required beyond confirmation
of the $50,000 threshold.

### Form 990-EZ

**Eligibility:** Gross receipts < $200,000 AND total assets < $500,000. Short-form return
with simplified reporting.

### Form 990 (Full)

**Required when:** Gross receipts ≥ $200,000 OR total assets ≥ $500,000. Includes:

- **Part I:** Summary of financial and operational data
- **Part III:** Program service accomplishments (3 largest programs)
- **Part IV:** Checklist of required schedules
- **Part V:** Statements regarding other IRS filings and tax compliance
- **Part VI:** Governance, management, and disclosure — board composition, policies
- **Part VII:** Compensation of officers, directors, trustees, key employees
- **Part VIII:** Statement of revenue — contributions, program service, investment income
- **Part IX:** Statement of functional expenses — program, management & general, fundraising
- **Part X:** Balance sheet — assets, liabilities, net assets
- **Part XI:** Reconciliation of net assets
- **Part XII:** Financial statements and reporting

### Key Schedules

| Schedule | When Required |
|----------|--------------|
| Schedule A | Public charity status and public support |
| Schedule B | Schedule of contributors (detailed donor list to IRS only) |
| Schedule C | Political campaign and lobbying activities |
| Schedule D | Supplemental financial statements |
| Schedule F | Activities outside the United States |
| Schedule G | Fundraising events and gaming |
| Schedule I | Grants and other assistance |
| Schedule J | Compensation information |
| Schedule O | Supplemental information (narrative explanations) |
| Schedule R | Related organizations and unrelated partnerships |

### Filing Deadlines & Extensions

- Due 15th day of 5th month after fiscal year end (May 15 for calendar-year filers)
- Automatic 6-month extension via Form 8868 (November 15 for calendar-year)
- Additional 3-month non-automatic extension available
- Late filing penalties: $20/day (990-N returns exempt from penalty)

### Public Disclosure Requirements

Forms 990, 990-EZ, 990-T, and Form 1023 must be made available for public inspection
during regular business hours. Also available via IRS.gov, GuideStar/Candid, and
ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer. Schedule B donor information is NOT publicly disclosable.

## Maintaining Tax-Exempt Status

### Automatic Revocation

Failure to file Form 990 series for three consecutive years results in automatic
revocation of tax-exempt status. Reinstatement requires re-application (Form 1023/1024)
and payment of user fee.

### Unrelated Business Income Tax (UBIT)

Income from trade or business that is regularly carried on and not substantially related
to the exempt purpose is subject to UBIT. Reported on Form 990-T.

- **Substantially related:** Activity contributes importantly to accomplishing exempt
  purposes, other than generating income
- **Exclusions from UBIT:** Activities conducted substantially by volunteers, sale of
  donated merchandise, qualified convention activities, qualified sponsorship payments
- **Silo rule (post-2017):** Each separate trade or business must calculate UBIT separately
  — losses from one business cannot offset income from another
- **Exclusion threshold:** $1,000 gross income from unrelated business

### Excess Benefit Transactions

Intermediate sanctions (IRC §4958) impose excise taxes on disqualified persons who receive
excess benefits and on organization managers who knowingly approve them. Penalties: 25% of
excess benefit (up to 200% if not corrected), 10% on managers (up to $20,000 per transaction).

### Private Inurement / Private Benefit

- **Inurement:** No part of net earnings may inure to the benefit of any private
  shareholder or individual. Absolute prohibition.
- **Private benefit:** More than incidental private benefit to individuals or
  organizations jeopardizes exempt status. Qualitative and quantitative analysis required.

## State Compliance

### Charitable Solicitation Registration

Most states require registration before soliciting charitable contributions. Typically
involves filing with the state Attorney General or Secretary of State's office. Renewal
usually annual. Unified registration available through Multi-State Filer Project for
participating states.

### State Tax Exemption

Separate from IRS determination. States may grant: sales tax exemption, property tax
exemption, corporate income tax exemption, franchise tax exemption. Each requires separate
application.

### Annual State Filings

Most states require an annual report or renewal. Track deadlines for each state of
incorporation and each state where the organization conducts activities.

## International Grantmaking

### Expenditure Responsibility

Required when making grants to foreign organizations that are not U.S. equivalents of
public charities. Five-step process:

1. Pre-grant inquiry: verify grantee's charitable purpose and financial capacity
2. Written agreement: specify charitable purposes, reporting requirements, and
   prohibition on diversion of funds
3. Segregated account: require grantee to maintain grant funds separately
4. Regular reports: receive and review reports at least annually
5. Reporting to IRS: disclose expenditure responsibility grants on Form 990 Schedule F

### Equivalency Determination

Process of determining that a foreign organization is the equivalent of a U.S. public
charity. Requires affidavit or opinion of counsel based on review of foreign organization's
governing documents, financial data, and operations. Avoids expenditure responsibility
requirements.

### OFAC Compliance

Verify that grantees and individuals are not on OFAC Specially Designated Nationals list.
Anti-terrorist financing due diligence is legally required.
