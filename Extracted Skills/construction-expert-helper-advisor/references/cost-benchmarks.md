# Haiti Construction Cost Benchmarks Reference

Data collected from deep research in April 2025-2026. Costs are in USD.

**IMPORTANT:** These figures reflect 2025-26 market conditions. If the current date is more than 12 months after April 2026, note this explicitly in any cost estimate and recommend the user verify prices with local suppliers. Haiti's material costs are volatile due to import dependency, exchange rate fluctuations, and security conditions affecting transport.

## Overall Construction Cost Ranges

| Category | Low | Mid | High | Notes |
|----------|-----|-----|------|-------|
| Engineered residential (per sq ft) | $75 | $100 | $125+ | Confined masonry, full MEP, proper finishes |
| Basic residential (per sq ft) | $75 | $90 | $100 | Minimal finishes, basic electrical |
| Mid-spec residential (per sq ft) | $90 | $110 | $120 | Standard finishes, full plumbing and electrical |
| High-spec residential (per sq ft) | $110 | $125 | $150+ | Premium finishes, imported fixtures |

**Cost structure:** Materials = ~85% of total cost, labor = ~10-15%. This is inverted from US/European norms where labor dominates. Consequence: material price swings hit Haitian budgets harder than labor inefficiency.

**NGO baseline comparisons ($14-$33/sq ft) are NOT valid benchmarks** for engineered construction. Those figures typically reflect unreinforced masonry with no rebar, no MEP, and no finishes.

## High-Cost Items to Flag

| Item | Estimated Cost | Why It's Expensive |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| PVC bronze-glass windows | $4,000-$7,500 total | Likely imported from Port-au-Prince or Dominican Republic; long lead time |
| Material transport to Fond-des-Blancs | $2,000-$5,000 | 3-5 hour trip from PaP; security surcharges 30-50%+ on freight |
| Cement (150-200 bags for a house) | $1,200-$1,800 | $2/bag price swing = $300-$400 budget impact |
| Construction water (if no on-site source) | $500-$2,000 | Trucked water is expensive; plan for cistern or well |
| Steel tube roof trusses | $1,500-$3,000 | Fabricated in PaP, transported to site |

## Import and Tax Context

- Import duties: 17-27% on construction materials
- All steel, most electrical/plumbing fittings, and specialty items are imported
- Port bottlenecks in Port-au-Prince can add 1-4 weeks to delivery
- Security conditions on Route Nationale 2 (PaP to south) affect transport scheduling and cost

## Contingency Guidance

- Minimum contingency: 15% of estimated total
- Recommended contingency: 20% for projects in Fond-des-Blancs
- Additional buffer for: cement price volatility, transport disruptions, weather delays, design changes during construction
