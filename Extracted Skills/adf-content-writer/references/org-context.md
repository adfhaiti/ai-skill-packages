# ADF Haiti: Organizational Context, Finances, Governance, and Milestones

## Financial Trajectory (2020-2024)

ADF Haiti's financial story is a pivot from grant-dependence toward diversified revenue. This trajectory is central to donor communications and grant narratives.

| Year | Total Revenue | Grants | Earned Income | Individual Donations | Key Driver |
|------|--------------|--------|---------------|---------------------|------------|
| 2020 | ~$209K | ~$187K (90%) | ~$12K (6%) | ~$8K (4%) | WKKF grant funding |
| 2021 | ~$369K | ~$340K (92%) | ~$22K (6%) | ~$7K (2%) | Peak WKKF year |
| 2022 | ~$195K | ~$15K (8%) | ~$135K (69%) | ~$43K (22%) | WKKF grants ended; GIS contracts filled gap |
| 2023 | ~$306K | ~$136K (44%) | ~$111K (36%) | ~$55K (18%) | Balanced mix: new grants + strong contracts |
| 2024 | ~$126K | ~$48K (38%) | ~$17K (13%) | ~$60.5K (48%) | Low year; individual giving became largest source |

Key narratives for content writers:
- Foundation grants collapsed 86% between 2021 and 2024 (from $340K to $48K)
- Individual giving grew 800%+ (from under $10K to $60.5K)
- Earned revenue peaked at $111K in 2023 (became largest single source that year)
- ADF survived the loss of its primary funder by leaning on its services and community support
- Target: 60% earned revenue by 2029; no single funder exceeding 20-25% of budget

When writing about ADF's finances for donors or funders, use this data to show resilience and strategic adaptation, not fragility.

## Board of Directors (Key Profiles)

The board is elected by ADF's general assembly of 60+ member organizations. All members are Haitian. The board operates under the Policy Governance Model.

**Dr. Inobert Pierre, Chairperson:** Pediatrician and Director General of St. Boniface Hospital (Health Equity International) in Fond-des-Blancs. Grew up in Cap-Haitien. Provides strategic oversight and deep community trust. His healthcare leadership experience informs ADF's community development approach.

**Michelet Mascary, Vice Chair:** Multilingual development expert with degrees in agriculture (Panama) and animal husbandry (Puerto Rico). Former OAS and IICA consultant. Currently teaches at Haitian universities and runs a farm in Fond-des-Blancs. Provides expertise in agriculture and economic development.

**Jean Thomas, Secretary:** Veteran community leader in Fond-des-Blancs since 1982. Founder of Haiti Christian Development Fund (HCDF). Background in theology and community ministry (educated in the US). Among the local visionaries who inspired ADF's creation. Provides institutional memory and mentorship.

**Ferry Cadet, Board Member:** Haitian-American with an MBA. Experience at the Haiti Fund at the Boston Foundation and as Operations Manager at a community foundation. Brings diaspora perspective, grantmaking expertise, and gender equity focus.

**Additional female board members (from Haitian Leaders Impact Fund application):** Marie-Helene Amonard, Rose-Naomie Argant, Amantha Bonhomme. The board has three women out of nine members, as mandated by ADF's electoral rules (one-third representation).

## Mityel Solidarite (Rotating Credit Fund)

Launched in 2023 in partnership with local groups. Provides small loans/grants to member organizations for sustainable projects. Addresses capital access in an area where traditional financing is scarce. First-year results: financed at least 3 local projects (community grain mill, school canteen, irrigation pump). This is ADF's first move into the micro-finance space.

## Core Values (from Organizational Context Document)

1. **Local Leadership:** Prioritize Haitian-led decision-making and community ownership
2. **Data-Driven Action:** Evidence-based interventions using surveys and GIS
3. **Capacity Building (specific):** Training local organizations so they can design and implement their own solutions
4. **Collaboration and Networking:** ADF as hub for coordination and information-sharing
5. **Sustainable Development:** Systems and infrastructure that continue beyond any single project
