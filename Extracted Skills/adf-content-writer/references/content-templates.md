# ADF Content Writer: Content Type Templates

These scaffolds provide structural guidance for the most common output types. Adapt the structure to the specific request; these are starting frameworks, not rigid forms.

## 1. Donor Appeal / Fundraising Email

```
Subject: [Specific outcome or milestone, not vague]

Hi [First Name],

[Opening: one concrete, specific scene or fact from the field. No background preamble.]

[1-2 sentences: why this matters. Connect to ADF's theory of change.]

[What's next / what the gap is. Honest, specific.]

[The ask: specific dollar amount tied to a tangible deliverable.]
"Your $[amount] [does specific thing]."

[Brief close: investment framing, not gratitude-heavy.]

Josiah Thomas
Executive Director, ADF Haiti
```

**Rules:**
- First person (Josiah voice)
- Two accomplishments maximum
- Cause-and-effect: problem, gift, outcome
- Specific dollar tiers from Roots & Wings or project-specific costs
- No comprehensive reporting; this is a focused update
- Never: poverty imagery, savior narrative, "despite the challenges"

## 2. Program Description (for proposals, website, reports)

```
[Program name]

[1-2 sentences: what it is and what it does. Active voice, present tense.]

[Scope: specific numbers, geographic coverage, population served.]

[Methodology / approach: tools, techniques, team structure.]

[Outcomes / impact: measurable results with specific data points.]

[Connection to ADF's mission: how this program contributes to the theory of change.]
```

**Rules:**
- Confident voice: "ADF conducts..." not "It is proposed that..."
- Name specific tools (Fulcrum, ArcGIS Pro, Power BI)
- Name specific partners and client organizations
- Include numbers: households, schools, parcels, certifications earned
- No generic jargon ("capacity building" becomes "training 50 teachers on classroom management")

## 3. Newsletter Section / Blog Post

```
[Headline: lead with a concrete outcome or action, not background]

[Opening paragraph: a specific story, data point, or milestone. Hook the reader immediately.]

[2-3 body paragraphs:
  - What happened (specific project, specific results)
  - Why it matters (connected to the larger mission)
  - What's next (forward-looking)]

[Closing: one sentence connecting to the reader's role or a call to action]
```

**Rules:**
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
- At least one specific data point per post
- Lead with outcome, not background
- End with forward-looking statement
- Bilingual project names where natural (Pwoje Bright Seed, Kore Vwazen)

## 4. Social Media Post (Short-Form)

```
[Hook: one strong statement, question, or contrast. Under 20 words.]

[Body: 1-3 sentences expanding with a specific fact or outcome.]

[CTA or link (if applicable)]

[Hashtags: 3-5 relevant tags]
```

**Platform guidance:**
- LinkedIn: 100-200 words, professional/B2B when targeting clients; mission-focused when targeting supporters
- Facebook/Instagram: 50-150 words, more narrative, visual language
- X/Twitter: under 280 characters; one fact, one message

**Hashtag bank:** #ADFHaiti #FondDesBlancs #HaitiDevelopment #LocalLeadership #DataForDignity #EkiTek #HaitiGIS #SelfDetermination #PostAid

**Rules:**
- One message per post
- Concrete over abstract ("50 schools" not "dozens of schools")
- Moderate emoji use (contextual, not decorative)

## 5. Grant Narrative Paragraph

```
[Context sentence: the specific need or gap in Fond-des-Blancs.]

[ADF's approach: what ADF will do, with specifics (team size, tools, timeline).]

[Expected outcomes: measurable indicators tied to the grant's objectives.]

[Sustainability: how results persist after the grant period, connecting to ADF's earned-revenue model or local institutional capacity.]
```

**Rules:**
- Confident: "ADF will deploy..." not "ADF proposes to potentially..."
- Specific deliverables, timelines, and responsible parties
- Theory of change clearly threaded through
- Reference WKKF narrative voice as gold standard (see `grant-voice.md`)
- No hedging; state capacity and track record directly

## 6. Organizational Overview / Boilerplate

**Short (50 words):**
ADF Haiti is a Haitian-led nonprofit that sells data collection, GIS mapping, and program evaluation services to fund community development in Fond-des-Blancs. With 60+ member organizations and 10-12 local staff, ADF strengthens civil society and coordinates development, measuring success not by aid delivered, but by aid no longer required.

**Medium (100 words):**
ADF Haiti (Association pour le Developpement de Fond-des-Blancs) is a Haitian-led 501(c)(3) nonprofit with an all-Haitian Board of Directors and 10-12 staff from Fond-des-Blancs. Founded in 2015, ADF sells professional data collection, GIS/drone mapping, and program evaluation services to NGOs, donors, and government agencies. Revenue funds community development across a network of 60+ civil society organizations serving approximately 100,000 people. ADF also operates EkiTek Academy, Haiti's only Certiport and ICDL accredited testing center for digital skills. ADF measures success not by aid delivered, but by aid no longer required.

**Long (200 words):**
ADF Haiti (Association pour le Developpement de Fond-des-Blancs) is a Haitian-led 501(c)(3) nonprofit headquartered in Fond-des-Blancs, Haiti's Sud Department. Founded in 2015 with support from the W.K. Kellogg Foundation, ADF employs 10-12 staff, all from Fond-des-Blancs, and is governed by an all-Haitian Board of Directors elected by a general assembly of 60+ member civil society organizations.

ADF's core model is distinctive: it sells professional data collection, GIS/drone mapping, and program evaluation services to NGOs, bilateral donors, local government, and businesses. This earned revenue funds mission-driven community development, reducing dependency on grants. Service capabilities include Fulcrum-based survey deployment, Eos Arrow Gold RTK GNSS positioning, DJI Phantom 4 Pro drone imagery, and Power BI data visualization.

ADF coordinates the REFZAEQ school network (50 schools, 8,000+ students, 500+ teachers) and supports organizations spanning education, agriculture, health, and economic development. Through EkiTek Academy, Haiti's first and only Certiport Authorized Testing Center and ICDL Accredited Testing Center, ADF offers internationally recognized digital skills certifications.

ADF's mission: strengthen local organizations and coordinate their work toward an empowered Fond-des-Blancs. Success is measured not by aid delivered, but by aid no longer required.

## Architect of Autonomy Framework

The Product Marketing strategy document establishes ADF's brand voice as the "Architect of Autonomy." This framework has three programmatic narratives that can be used across content types:

1. **"Data as Dignity" (GIS and Research):** Data collection is reframed as "Putting Fond-des-Blancs on the Map." Mapping invisible communities gives them rights and resources. "Precision as Protection."

2. **"Opportunity through Education" (EkiTek):** EkiTek addresses the "Heartbreaking Choice" of leaving home for opportunity. It acts as a "Bridge to the World" by offering international certifications. Impact: "Keeping Families Together."

3. **"Legacy in Agriculture" (HCDF/Jubilee Farms):** GPS mapping of farmlands turns "occupied land" into "family legacy." Addresses the "Fear of Loss" and ensures "Peace of Mind" for farmers.

**Important calibration note:** The Product Marketing doc uses more promotional language than ADF's core voice. When using these narratives, apply them through the anti-AI style filter: no flowery language, no metaphorical excess, no "empower" or "foster." Use the framing concepts (Data as Dignity, Bridge to the World, Legacy in Agriculture) but express them in ADF's professionally casual, specific, direct voice.
