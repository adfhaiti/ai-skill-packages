# ADF Haiti: Grant Narrative Voice Guide

## Tone Calibration

ADF's successful grants (WKKF, HDI, Peace Development Fund, LASC) share these voice characteristics:

- **Confident and declarative.** "ADF will deploy a team of 26 enumerators across three communes" not "ADF proposes to potentially deploy..."
- **Specific and quantified.** Every claim backed by a number, a name, or a date.
- **Theory-of-change driven.** The narrative connects activities to intermediate outcomes to long-term impact.
- **Locally grounded.** References to specific places (Fond-des-Blancs, Torbeck, Les Cayes, Aquin), specific organizations (REFZAEQ, HFSA), and specific people.
- **Honest about gaps.** ADF does not oversell. When no data exists, it says so ("No organization or government entity conducts demographic or socioeconomic research in Fond-des-Blancs").

## Structure Pattern (from WKKF narratives)

1. **Context:** Brief, factual description of the situation in Fond-des-Blancs. No pity framing. Focus on structural gaps (lack of data, lack of coordination, weak government presence).
2. **ADF's role and track record:** What ADF has already done, with specifics. Past contracts, partnerships, concrete deliverables completed.
3. **Proposed activities:** Clear deliverables, timelines, responsible parties. Active voice throughout.
4. **Expected outcomes:** Measurable indicators. Tied back to the theory of change.
5. **Sustainability:** How the work continues after the grant. This is where ADF's earned-revenue model and local ownership are strongest.

## Theory of Change (for grant narratives)

**If** local civil society organizations are strengthened and coordinated, **and** the community has reliable data about its own needs, **then** Haitians will hold the decision-making power over their own development, **resulting in** sustainable community transformation that does not depend on external aid.

ADF's specific contributions to this chain:
- Data collection and research fills the information gap
- Organizational development strengthens individual CSOs
- Coordination and planning align CSO efforts toward shared goals
- Digital skills training (EkiTek) builds individual human capital
- Earned revenue from services reduces dependency on grants

## Key Differentiators (for competitive grant applications)

- All-Haitian staff, all from Fond-des-Blancs
- All-Haitian Board of Directors, elected by member organizations
- 60+ member civil society organizations in the network
- 8+ years of data collection experience
- Only ICDL Accredited and Certiport Authorized Testing Center in Haiti
- Equipment: Eos Arrow Gold RTK GNSS, DJI Phantom 4 Pro v2.0, ArcGIS Pro, Fulcrum
- Track record: HDI (85 schools, 14,000 students), Acceso (1,500+ parcels), Bracing Neighbors (16,000+ households)

## Project Bright Seed (WKKF) Reference

The most recent major grant narrative is for Project Bright Seed (Pwoje Bright Seed), funded by the W.K. Kellogg Foundation. Phase 1 covered: ICDL accreditation, digital literacy pilot, sports governance, teacher professional development, financial sustainability planning, and education partner selection. Phase 2 is under consideration.

This narrative is the gold standard for ADF's grant voice. When writing new grant narratives, calibrate against it.
