## Hiring & Recruitment

### Defining the Role

Before posting, define: job purpose and key responsibilities, required vs. preferred
qualifications, competencies and attributes for success, reporting structure, career
path, and compensation range. Avoid credential inflation — define what's truly
necessary for job performance.

### Building the Candidate Pool

- Post in mission-aligned channels, not just general job boards
- Leverage networks of staff, board, and volunteers
- Proactively recruit candidates who reflect the community served
- Consider internal candidates and program alumni
- Use consistent screening criteria applied to all candidates

### Selection Process

1. Resume/application review against defined criteria
2. Initial screen (phone/video, 20-30 min)
3. In-depth interviews (competency-based, behavioral questions)
4. Work sample, case study, or presentation relevant to the role
5. Reference checks (at least 3, including past supervisors)
6. Background check (per policy and position sensitivity)

### Behavioral Interviewing

Past behavior is the best predictor of future performance. Questions structured as:
"Tell me about a time when you..." followed by probing for Situation, Task, Action,
Result. Rate candidates on defined competencies, not general impressions.

### Onboarding

Comprehensive 90-day plan including: organizational orientation (mission, history,
culture), role-specific training, introduction to key stakeholders, and clear
expectations with 30/60/90-day milestones. Assign a peer buddy. The executive
director should personally onboard all direct reports.

## Performance Management

### Setting Expectations (Green & Hauser, 2012)

- **SMART goals:** Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
- **Role clarity:** Clear role descriptions with decision authority boundaries
- **Annual goals:** Derived from strategic plan, linked to organizational priorities
- **Regular check-ins:** Weekly one-on-ones are essential for alignment, coaching,
  and course correction

### Weekly One-on-Ones

Structure: employee's agenda first (what they need), then manager's updates,
priorities review, coaching/development. Not status reporting (use other
mechanisms for that). Focus on: obstacles, decisions needed, development,
and relationship building.

### Performance Evaluations

Annual formal evaluation based on: goal achievement, competency demonstration,
values alignment, and contribution to team and organization. Include self-assessment
component. Separate evaluation conversation from compensation conversation
(conduct at different times to avoid defensive positioning).

### 360-Degree Feedback

Collect feedback from supervisor, peers, direct reports, and external stakeholders
(as appropriate). Best used for development, not evaluation decisions. Requires
organizational maturity and psychological safety to implement effectively.

## Compensation Strategy

### IRS Rebuttable Presumption of Reasonableness

Compensation is presumed reasonable if approved by independent board (or committee),
based on appropriate comparability data (compensation surveys, Form 990 review of
similar organizations), and documented contemporaneously. Shifts burden of proof
to IRS.

### Total Rewards Approach

Consider total compensation, not just salary: base pay, health and retirement
benefits, paid time off, professional development, flexible work arrangements,
mission-aligned culture, and work-life integration. Nonprofit compensation should
be fair, not sacrificial.

### Compensation Philosophy

Articulate: position relative to market (lead, match, lag), pay for performance
principles, transparency level, equity considerations (internal equity and external
competitiveness), and relationship to mission and values.

### Compliance

- Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA): proper exemption classification (executive,
  administrative, professional exemptions)
- State and local minimum wage, paid leave, and pay transparency laws
- Form 990 Part VII and Schedule J disclosure requirements
- No private inurement; compensation must serve organizational purposes

## Inclusion & Equity

### Total Inclusion Management (Bernstein & Salipante, 2024)

Move beyond diversity (representation) to inclusion (full participation) and equity
(fair outcomes). Framework includes: leadership commitment, equitable HR practices,
inclusive culture and interactions, accountability systems, and stakeholder voice.

### Equitable HR Practices

- Bias-interrupting hiring (diverse hiring panels, structured interviews, skill-based
  assessments)
- Pay equity analysis (audit for gender, race, and other disparities)
- Promotion and advancement transparency
- Inclusive benefits (consider diverse family structures, accessibility)
- Accommodation processes that are proactive, not adversarial

## Employment Law Compliance

### Classification

- **Employee vs. independent contractor:** Multi-factor test (behavioral control,
  financial control, relationship type). Misclassification carries significant
  penalties.
- **Exempt vs. non-exempt:** Salary basis test, salary level test ($35,568/year
  federal; state may be higher), duties test. Non-exempt employees must receive
  overtime at 1.5× for hours over 40 per week.

### Key Employment Policies

Employee handbook covering: at-will employment statement, anti-discrimination and
anti-harassment, reasonable accommodation, leave policies (FMLA, state paid leave,
organization policy), confidentiality, code of conduct, technology use, and
complaint procedures.

## Staff Development & Retention

### Development Methods (Green & Hauser, 2012)

- **Stretch assignments:** Projects beyond current role that develop new capabilities
- **Coaching:** Real-time feedback and skill development by manager
- **Formal training:** Workshops, courses, conferences relevant to role
- **Peer learning:** Communities of practice, cross-functional teams
- **Mentoring:** Experienced guide for career and professional development

### Retention Strategies

- Competitive, fair compensation
- Meaningful work connected to mission
- Clear career paths and growth opportunities
- Positive, inclusive culture
- Work-life integration and flexibility
- Appreciation and recognition
- Investment in professional development

**Red flags for retention risk:** plateau (no growth for 2+ years), compensation
significantly below market, poor manager relationship, organizational instability,
values misalignment.

## Volunteer Management

### Volunteer Program Design (Nesbit, 2024)

- **Role design:** Meaningful roles with clear expectations, not make-work.
  Distinguish: direct service, administrative, governance, skilled/pro bono,
  episodic, virtual.
- **Recruitment:** Target based on role requirements; use volunteer matching
  platforms, partner organizations, current volunteer referrals.
- **Screening:** Application, interview, background check (per role), reference
  check. Screening depth proportional to role risk.
- **Training and orientation:** Organization mission and values, role-specific
  skills, policies and procedures, boundaries, safety.

### Volunteer Management Cycle

1. **Recruitment and placement**
2. **Orientation and training**
3. **Engagement and supervision** — regular check-ins, support, resources
4. **Recognition and retention** — formal (awards, events) and informal
   (thank you notes, program impact sharing)
5. **Evaluation** — volunteer satisfaction, performance, program effectiveness
6. **Offboarding and alumni engagement**

### Managing Volunteers vs. Staff

Volunteers require: greater flexibility, different motivational levers (mission
connection, community, skill development, purpose), more frequent recognition,
and clear boundaries (volunteers supplement — not replace — staff functions).
The most common failure mode: treating volunteers as unpaid staff with the same
expectations and management approach.

## Managing Performance Problems

### Identifying Issues

Distinguish: skill issue (can be trained), will issue (motivation/engagement),
fit issue (role misalignment), and capacity issue (overwhelmed). Treatment
differs for each.

### Progressive Discipline

1. **Verbal coaching:** Private, specific, documented. Clarify expectations.
2. **Written warning:** Formal documentation, improvement plan with specific
   measurable goals and timeline, consequences stated.
3. **Final warning / Performance Improvement Plan (PIP):** Intensive support
   with clear deadline for improvement.
4. **Termination:** When improvement doesn't occur or for serious violations.

### Termination Best Practices

- Consult employment attorney before termination
- Document performance issues throughout
- Conduct termination meeting with witness present
- Prepare: final paycheck (per state law), benefits information, separation agreement
  if applicable
- Communicate to team with dignity (respect privacy, don't share details)
- Offboard: IT access, keys, return of property checklist
