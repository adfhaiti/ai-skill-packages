# Capability Statement

## Document Structure

A capability statement is a standalone 1-2 page organizational profile used for EOIs,
pre-qualification submissions, and general marketing. Structure in 6 sections.

### Section 1: Header Block

- ADF logo (top left)
- Document title: "Capability Statement" or "Organizational Profile"
- Tagline: "Haitian-Led Data Collection, GIS Mapping, and Program Evaluation Services"
- Prussian Blue (#0a3d50) header bar with white text

### Section 2: Organizational Overview

2-3 paragraphs covering:

"ADF Haiti (Association pour le Developpement de Fond-des-Blancs) is a Haitian-founded
and Haitian-led 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization headquartered in Fond-des-Blancs, Sud
Department, Haiti. Founded in 2015, ADF operates a for-profit technical services division
whose revenue funds community development programs, creating a sustainable model
independent of grant cycles.

ADF employs 10-12 full-time staff, all from Fond-des-Blancs and surrounding communities.
The organization is governed by an all-Haitian Board of Directors. ADF has delivered
data collection, GIS mapping, and evaluation services to international NGOs, bilateral
donors, the Haitian government, and private sector clients since its founding."

### Section 3: Core Competencies

Present as a compact 2-column or 3-column layout:

**Data Collection Services**
- Household and community surveys
- Needs assessments and baseline studies
- Census and enumeration
- Program monitoring data collection

**GIS and Mapping Services**
- RTK GNSS parcel surveys (sub-5cm accuracy)
- Drone mapping and aerial surveys
- Spatial database development
- Web mapping and dashboard creation

**Program Evaluation**
- Organizational Capacity Assessments
- Program M&E design and implementation
- Baseline, midline, and endline studies
- Data analysis and visualization

**Training and Capacity Building**
- Digital skills training (via EkiTek Academy)
- Drone operations certification
- GIS and data collection training
- Organizational capacity strengthening

## Formatting Rules

- Maximum 2 pages (1 page preferred for EOI attachments)
- Aptos 10pt body text (smaller than standard 11pt to fit content)
- ADF brand colors: Prussian Blue headers, Pale Blue Lily section backgrounds,
  Smooth Green for highlights
- No em dashes
- Landscape orientation acceptable for 1-page versions
- Include ADF logo; do not include photos unless specifically requested

## Tailoring Rules

When a capability statement responds to a specific EOI:

1. Reorder core competencies to lead with the service the EOI requests
2. Select past performance examples most relevant to the EOI scope
3. Add a brief "Relevant Experience" paragraph connecting ADF to the specific requirement
4. Adjust NAICS codes to highlight the most relevant code first
5. If the EOI specifies a format, follow it exactly; use this structure as fallback

## Version Types

### Full (2 Pages)
All 6 sections with expanded past performance and competency descriptions.

### One-Pager
Compressed layout: header, 2-sentence overview, competencies as bullet clusters,
3-project past performance table, org data block. Landscape orientation recommended.

### Paragraph Version (100-200 words)
For insertion into larger documents or email responses:

"ADF Haiti (Association pour le Developpement de Fond-des-Blancs) is a Haitian-led
501(c)(3) nonprofit providing professional data collection, GIS/drone mapping, and
program evaluation services from its base in Fond-des-Blancs, Sud Department, Haiti.
With 10-12 full-time Haitian staff, ADF owns Arrow Gold RTK GNSS receivers (sub-5cm
accuracy), DJI Phantom 4 drones, and operates the Fulcrum and ArcGIS platforms for
field data collection and spatial analysis. ADF has delivered services to USAID-funded
programs (via HDI), ACCESO, FECCANO, Welthungerhilfe, and other international and
local clients, with individual project values ranging from $5,000 to $150,000. ADF also
operates EkiTek Academy, a digital skills training center offering certified instruction
in GIS, drone operations, and data collection."
