# Real-World HC Translation Examples

Annotated translation pairs drawn from ADF Haiti staff WhatsApp messages (2,551 messages, 39 participants, Fond-des-Blancs 2026). Each example includes the original HC, the English translation, and notes on grammar, register, or translation choices.

## Scheduling and Logistics

The most common message category in professional HC messaging. Characterized by short sentences, progressive marker ("ap"), and time references.

### Arriving Late

**HC**: M ap ontijan anreta maten an
**EN**: I'll be a bit late this morning
**Notes**: "ontijan" (fused form of "on ti jan") = "a little bit." Progressive "ap" here signals near future, not ongoing action. "maten an" = "this morning" (definite article "an" after nasal vowel).

**HC**: Map gon ti reta maten an
**EN**: I'm going to be a bit late this morning
**Notes**: "Map" = fused "M ap" (informal register). "gon" = informal contraction of "gen yon." "ti reta" = "a little delay."

**HC**: Map anreta pou kek minit
**EN**: I'll be late by a few minutes
**Notes**: "kek" = "quelques" (a few). Direct, no hedging.

### Scheduling Coordination

**HC**: Mwen gen pou m al lekol gaspa ak Felicita maten an ankò map nan biro a 10 hres.
**EN**: I need to go to Gaspa school with Felicita this morning; I'll be at the office at 10.
**Notes**: "gen pou m" = obligation ("have to / need to"). "hres" = informal abbreviation of French "heures." Two clauses joined without conjunction (typical HC messaging).

**HC**: Gen machin sen bonifas kap sot Okay demen?
**EN**: Is there a Saint-Boniface vehicle coming from Les Cayes tomorrow?
**Notes**: "kap" = fused "ki ap" (that is). "sot" = "coming from" (lit: "leaving"). "Okay" = HC spelling of Les Cayes. Question formed by intonation alone (no "eske").

**HC**: Felicita bezwen vitamin k. L ap chache nan lin isit yo men l poko jwenn.
**EN**: Felicita needs vitamin K. She's looking in the pharmacies here but hasn't found it yet.
**Notes**: "lin" = "ligne" (pharmacy/store, from French). "poko" = "not yet" (pa + ankò contracted). "jwenn" = to find/get.

**HC**: Dapre sa boss la dim lap remet machinn nan demen sDv
**EN**: According to what the boss told me, he'll return the car tomorrow, God willing
**Notes**: "dim" = fused "di m" (told me). "lap" = fused "l ap." "sDv" = "si Dye vle" (God willing), standard scheduling hedge.

### Availability / Attendance

**HC**: Ou gentan lòtbò a? Si pa gen moun nan wout map mache.
**EN**: Are you already on the other side? If there's nobody on the road, I'll walk.
**Notes**: "gentan" = "already" (one word, both AKA and Zefi agree). "lòtbò" = the other side (agglutinated). "map mache" = I'll walk (fused progressive).

**HC**: Kiyès k ap reponn prezan pou envitasyon Elson lan?
**EN**: Who's RSVPing for Elson's invitation?
**Notes**: "Kiyès k ap" = "Who is" (question word + progressive). "prezan" = "present" (responding yes). Article "lan" after nasal consonant.

**HC**: Shelove pap ka vini tou
**EN**: Shelove won't be able to come either
**Notes**: "pap" = "pa ap" (negative progressive) = won't be. "ka" = can/able. "tou" = also/either.

**HC**: M pa gen moun lakay mwen. Fò m ale tou wi
**EN**: I don't have anyone at my house. I have to go too.
**Notes**: "Fò" = "il faut" (must/have to). "wi" at end = affirmative particle adding emphasis ("indeed").

## Group Coordination

Messages directed at teams or subgroups. Often use @-mentions, imperatives, and direct address.

**HC**: Mesye tout nèg ki bezwen ale map rele nou, di moun yo nou gen yon urgence epi nou soti
**EN**: Guys, whoever needs to leave, I'll call you; tell people we have an emergency and we're stepping out
**Notes**: "mesye" = gentlemen (group address). "nèg" = man/person (neutral, not derogatory in HC). "urgence" = code-switched French, standard in HC. Imperative "di moun yo" (tell the people).

**HC**: Reponn Anshu please. Dil wi nou enterese ak sa
**EN**: Reply to Anshu please. Tell him yes, we're interested in that
**Notes**: "Dil" = fused "di l" (tell him/her). "enterese ak" = interested in.

**HC**: Ann ba l direksyon pa rele ankò
**EN**: Let's give him directions and stop calling
**Notes**: "Ann" = "let's" (imperative marker, one word). "pa rele ankò" = "don't call anymore."

**HC**: Montre Jony sa pou l pa bat ou
**EN**: Show Jony this so he doesn't scold you
**Notes**: "bat" here = scold/reprimand (not physical), context-dependent. "pou l pa" = so that he doesn't.

**HC**: Di fednel ekspre l fè
**EN**: Tell Fednel he did it on purpose
**Notes**: "ekspre" = on purpose/deliberately. Short, direct accusation typical of informal HC.

## Status Updates and Reports

Professional status reporting in HC messaging. Often uses numbered lists, French institutional terms, and summary structures.

**HC**: Antou sou 3 gwoup gen 13/21 Pass. Epi 8 Fail. Rete gwoup HSB a pou Mercredi
**EN**: In total across 3 groups, 13/21 passed. And 8 failed. The HSB group remains for Wednesday.
**Notes**: "Antou" = "en tout" (in total). Numeric data presented in compact format. "Rete" = remains/is left.

**HC**: Epil pwobab pou loreya soti nan gwoup jodi a. Gen youn ki fè 975/1000
**EN**: And the valedictorian will probably come from today's group. There's one who scored 975/1000
**Notes**: "Epil" = fused "Epi l" (and it). "loreya" = laureate/valedictorian. "pwobab" = probably (truncated from "pwobabman").

**HC**: Résumé jounen an: 1- m fè certificat de bonne vie et mœurs yo 2- m jwenn permis de fonctionnement
**EN**: Summary of the day: 1- I got the certificates of good standing 2- I got the operating permit
**Notes**: Code-switching: "Résumé," "certificat de bonne vie et mœurs," and "permis de fonctionnement" are French legal terms embedded in HC grammar. "m fè" = I did/got. "m jwenn" = I obtained.

**HC**: Bò kote Nou pwosesis legal ok pou res sak gen pou fèt yo lè Kellogg's komanse finanse lig la na fè yo
**EN**: On our end, the legal process is done; what's left to do, we'll do once Kellogg's starts funding the league
**Notes**: "Bò kote Nou" = on our side/as for us. "pou res sak gen pou fèt yo" = for the rest of what needs to be done. "na fè yo" = we'll do them (contracted "nou a" or "n ap").

## Reactions, Emotions, and Social Bonding

Short expressive messages that maintain group cohesion.

**HC**: Bagay sa pa senp
**EN**: This thing is no joke / This is serious
**Notes**: Idiomatic. "pa senp" = not simple, meaning serious/complicated. Common reaction to news.

**HC**: Move nèt. Se kòmsi pa gen Avyon.
**EN**: Terrible. It's as if there are no planes.
**Notes**: "Move nèt" = bad completely / truly awful. "kòmsi" = as if (one word).

**HC**: Dyab ki manje l
**EN**: What the hell (lit: the devil that ate it)
**Notes**: Exclamation of frustration/surprise. Colloquial, not literal.

**HC**: Sanble se fake news
**EN**: Looks like it's fake news
**Notes**: "Sanble" = it seems/looks like. English "fake news" borrowed directly.

**HC**: Misye sanble se nou menm ki pou dil men kisa pou l fè.
**EN**: Dude, it seems like we're the ones who have to tell him, but what should he do.
**Notes**: "Misye" = informal address (from "monsieur" but fully creolized). "nou menm ki pou" = we ourselves who must. Cleft construction with "se...ki."

**HC**: Yon konprime banm ti elan la epi m abiye
**EN**: One pill gave me a little boost and I got dressed
**Notes**: "konprime" = pill/tablet. "elan" = momentum/boost. Serial verb implicit: took pill, got boost, got dressed.

## Requests and Directives

**HC**: Oke. M t ap pwopoze nou lage bonè jodi a.
**EN**: Okay. I was going to suggest we leave early today.
**Notes**: "t ap" = past progressive ("was going to"). "lage" = to release/leave (informal for depart). "bonè" = early.

**HC**: M ap fè w konnen.
**EN**: I'll let you know.
**Notes**: "fè w konnen" = make you know = inform you. "w" = short form of "ou." Standard professional closure.

**HC**: Voye link canva
**EN**: Send the Canva link
**Notes**: Bare imperative, no subject needed. "voye" = send. Direct, no hedging.

**HC**: Ann bay yon koudèy. Si tout bagay ok, poun ka pibliye.
**EN**: Let's take a look. If everything's okay, we can publish.
**Notes**: "Ann" = let's. "koudèy" = a glance/look (lit: a stroke of the eye). "poun" = fused "pou n" (for us to / so we can). "pibliye" = publish.
