# ADF Haiti: Key Messages

## Mission

Our mission is to strengthen local organizations and coordinate their work towards a shared vision of an empowered and economically competitive Fond-des-Blancs community.

## Vision

Our vision is the socio-economic transformation of Fond-des-Blancs into a globally attractive community where natives can lead prosperous lives.

## Global Ends Statement (Board-Level Purpose)

So long as value outweighs the cost, ADF exists so a growing number of community organizations can more effectively tackle barriers to Fond-des-Blancs being a model community.

Specifics under this:
1. Organizations exist to address agriculture, environmental, health, education, economy, and housing issues.
2. Organizations can establish solid legal foundations for receiving grants or can receive grants via ADF.
3. There is information and technology available to organizations regarding FdB's needs, granting opportunities, and organizational structures.
4. Information is shared between organizations.

## Goals

1. Increase the decision-making power that Haitians have over choices that impact their economic, cultural, and social development.
2. Strengthen local organizations so they can accomplish more and take on more responsibility.
3. Make it possible for all kids growing up in Fond-des-Blancs, now and in the future, to pursue and realize their full potential.

## Core Problem ADF Addresses

ADF's fundamental purpose is to address the lack of self-determination and decision-making power in the hands of Haitians. Haiti has been dubbed "A Republic of NGOs." The problem is not that NGOs render services; it is that the real decision-making power for practically all NGOs is in foreign hands. The result is that Haitians do not have decision-making power in many of the choices that impact their economic, cultural, and social development.

ADF combats this by thinking and hiring locally. Employing foreign workers is usually more expensive and often does not allow for knowledge transfer. ADF's entire staff is from Fond-des-Blancs. The Board of Directors is all-Haitian. Governance follows the "Policy Governance Model."

## Core Competencies

### 1. Research and Data Collection
No organization or government entity conducts demographic, socioeconomic, or any other type of research in Fond-des-Blancs. ADF fills this gap. The data ADF collects helps the community enact locally aware, systemic, and sustainable solutions.

### 2. Organizational Development and Member Services
ADF provides technical, financial, and in-kind support to local civic and nonprofit organizations, along with training on digital skills, management, and business administration.

### 3. Planning, Coordination, Collaboration, and Networking
- Planning: creating district-wide plans that guide civic and nonprofit operations
- Coordination: providing information about who is doing what, when, and where, along with GIS data
- Collaboration: creating opportunities for leaders to meet, form relationships, and share information

## Governance

ADF is governed by its membership (the majority of civil society organizations in Fond-des-Blancs). They form ADF's general assembly, which convenes annually. Each member votes in Board of Directors elections. Only official representatives of member organizations are eligible to serve on the nine-person Board. Board members serve three-year terms; elections are held in thirds annually. The Board operates under the Policy Governance Model. The Board hires/fires the CEO and defines organizational purpose, evaluates performance, and ensures cost-effective operations consistent with the mission.

## Articles of Incorporation Narrative

ADF Haiti, Inc. is a non-profit corporation organized exclusively for charitable, religious, educational, and scientific purposes. Our mission is to reinforce the capacity of local NGOs and coordinate and guide development in the greater Fond-des-Blancs area of Haiti. We help other nonprofit organizations do their jobs better by providing the types of logistical and reconnaissance support that are missing from many humanitarian aid and disaster relief projects underway in Haiti.
