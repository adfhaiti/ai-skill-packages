# GAAP vs IRS Reporting Differences

Critical differences between Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) and IRS Form 990 reporting rules. Organizations maintaining GAAP-basis financial statements must reconcile these differences when preparing Form 990. Schedule D Parts XI-XII provide the formal reconciliation mechanism.

Load this reference when preparing data from audited financial statements or when reconciliation issues arise between the books and the 990.

---

## Key Differences Table

| Subject | GAAP Treatment | Form 990 Treatment | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Donated services (volunteers)** | Must be recorded as revenue if services create/enhance nonfinancial assets OR require specialized skills (ASC 958-605) | May NOT be reported as revenue in Part VIII; reported only as memo on Part III or Schedule O | Audited financials show higher revenue than 990 |
| **Donated use of facilities** | Recognized at fair rental value | May NOT be reported as revenue in Part VIII | Same as above |
| **Consolidated statements** | Required if control/economic interest criteria met (SOP 94-3) | May NOT file consolidated; each entity files separately (except group exemption) | 990 cannot be prepared directly from consolidated financials |
| **Accounting changes** | Reflected retroactively by restating prior years | Must be shown as current year item on Part XI | Prior year comparisons may not match |
| **Prepaid fundraising costs** | Must be expensed in the period incurred | May be reported as assets (prepaid expenses) | Timing difference in expense recognition |
| **Investments** | Most must be reported at fair value (ASC 958-320) | May be reported at either cost or fair value | Balance sheet amounts may differ |
| **Unrealized gains/losses** | Reported on statement of activities as change in net assets | NOT reported as revenue in Part VIII; reconciled on Schedule D Part XII | Revenue figures differ between audit and 990 |
| **Pledges receivable** | Unconditional pledges recorded at present value when promised (ASC 958-605) | Cash-method orgs record only when received; accrual-method orgs follow GAAP | Revenue timing may differ for cash-basis filers |
| **Grants made (expense)** | Recognized when unconditional promise to pay is made (ASC 958-605) | Same for accrual-basis filers; cash-basis filers record when paid | Expense timing may differ |
| **Net asset classes** | Three classes required: without donor restrictions, with donor restrictions (ASC 958-210) | Not required on 990; Part X Lines 27-28 provide a two-class split | 990 provides less detail |
| **Functional expenses** | Required display for all nonprofits (ASC 958-720) | Required only for 501(c)(3) and 501(c)(4) organizations on Part IX | Scope differs |
| **Program expense detail** | Must show expenses separately for major program classes | Shows total for all programs together in Part IX Column B | Less granular on 990 |
| **Statement of cash flows** | Required for all organizations | Not required on Form 990 | No 990 equivalent |
| **Footnotes** | Required | Not required | 990 relies on Schedule O for supplemental disclosures |
| **Related party transactions** | Disclosure of all material items required (ASC 850) | Required only for specific categories: 501(c)(3) with non-c(3)s, or org with its disqualified persons | Narrower scope on 990 |
| **Number of years** | Not specified (normally same for all statements) | 2 years balance sheet; 1 year revenue/expenses in detail | Prior year shown only on Part I and Part X |
| **Investment expenses** | May be reported net of related revenue | Must be reported as expenses in Part IX (gross basis) | Revenue/expense figures differ |
| **Special event revenue** | All event revenue may be reported in one caption | Contribution portion separated from exchange portion (Part VIII Lines 1/8) | Revenue classification differs |

## Reconciliation Mechanics (Schedule D Parts XI-XII)

### Part XI: Reconciliation of Revenue per Audited Financial Statements with Revenue per Return

Common reconciliation items:
- **Add back**: donated services/facilities recorded on audited statements (not on 990)
- **Subtract**: unrealized gains reported on audited statements (not revenue on 990)
- **Subtract**: investment expenses netted against revenue on audited statements (reported gross on 990)
- **Adjust**: change in value of split-interest agreements
- **Adjust**: differences in contribution recognition timing (pledges)

### Part XII: Reconciliation of Expenses per Audited Financial Statements with Expenses per Return

Common reconciliation items:
- **Add back**: donated services/facilities expense recorded on audited statements
- **Adjust**: investment expenses netted on audited statements (reported gross on 990)
- **Adjust**: prior period adjustments
- **Adjust**: other differences in expense recognition

### When Reconciliation Is Required

Schedule D Parts XI-XII must be completed if the organization checked "Yes" on Part XII Line 2a (financial statements compiled, reviewed, or audited). If the accounting method and classifications are identical between the audited financials and the 990, the reconciliation lines will show $0 differences.

## Contribution Classification Rules

Understanding what counts as a "contribution" versus "program service revenue" is critical for both revenue reporting (Part VIII) and the public support test (Schedule A).

### Contributions (Part VIII Line 1)

One-way transfers motivated by detached and disinterested generosity:
- Cash donations from individuals, corporations, foundations
- Government grants where the grantor does not receive commensurate value
- In-kind donations of property (land, equipment, supplies, food)
- Bequests and testamentary gifts
- Corporate sponsorship payments treated as acknowledgments
- Cause-related marketing campaign proceeds (agency theory)

**NOT contributions:**
- Exchange transactions where both parties receive commensurate value
- Pass-through/agency funds (reported as liability, not revenue)
- Government contracts for specific deliverables (program service revenue)
- Membership dues attributable to purchased benefits (split: contribution portion + exchange portion)

### Pledge Recognition (Accrual Basis)

Unconditional pledges are recorded as revenue when promised (not when paid). Factors indicating a recordable pledge:
- Written evidence exists
- Language includes "promise," "agree," "will," or "binding"
- Payment dates are scheduled
- Donor has economic ability and history of timely payment
- Organization has taken action in reliance on pledge

Conditional pledges (containing "hope," "intend," "may," "expect") are NOT recorded until the condition is met. Example: a matching gift pledge is conditional until the match requirement is fulfilled.

Multi-year pledges: discount to present value; the annual increase (accretion) is reported as contribution income in each subsequent year. Provide an allowance for uncollectible pledges as a reduction of revenue (not an expense).

### Government Grants vs Contracts

This classification directly affects the public support test:
- **Government grant** (contribution, Part VIII Line 1e): the government provides funds to support the organization's mission with no specific deliverable required in return; the organization retains discretion over how funds are used within the grant purpose
- **Government contract** (program service revenue, Part VIII Line 2): the government purchases specific goods or services; the organization must deliver defined outputs; the arrangement has characteristics of a vendor/purchaser relationship

The distinction matters because contributions count as public support on Schedule A, while program service revenue may be limited (509(a)(1) test) or treated differently (509(a)(2) test).

### Donated Services: The Critical Distinction

- GAAP: specialized donated services must be recorded as both revenue and expense
- **Form 990: donated services may NOT be reported as revenue in Part VIII, regardless of GAAP treatment**
- The value of donated services is disclosed as a memo item only
- This creates a permanent difference between audited financials and the 990
- Schedule D Part XI reconciles this difference

However, in-kind donations of tangible property (not services) ARE reported on Part VIII Line 1g and must be valued at fair market value.

### Pass-Through / Agency Transactions

When an organization receives funds with an obligation to pass them to a designated recipient:
- **Agent with NO variance power**: funds are a liability, not revenue; report on Part X balance sheet only
- **Agent WITH variance power** (discretion over distribution): may recognize as revenue
- **Fiscally sponsored projects**: the sponsor IS the legal recipient; funds ARE revenue and expenses of the sponsor

This distinction is critical for the public support test: agency funds may need to be reported on Schedule A even though they are excluded from Part VIII revenue.

## Accounting Method Implications

### Cash vs Accrual

| Item | Cash Method | Accrual Method |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue recognition | When received | When earned/pledged |
| Expense recognition | When paid | When obligated |
| Pledges receivable | Not recorded | Recorded at present value |
| Accounts payable | Not recorded | Recorded when obligated |
| Grants payable | Not recorded | Recorded when promised |
| Balance sheet completeness | Less complete | More complete |

Once adopted, the accounting method cannot be changed without filing Form 3115 (Application for Change in Accounting Method).

**Exception**: a change required by FASB standards (e.g., adopting ASC 958) does not require Form 3115.

### Public Support Test Note

Schedule A support calculations use the same accounting method the organization uses for completing Form 990. Prior to the 2008 redesign, cash basis was required for support calculations regardless of the organization's accounting method. This is no longer the case.
