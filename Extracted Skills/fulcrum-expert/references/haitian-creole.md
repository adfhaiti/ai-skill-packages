# Haitian Creole Language Guide for Survey Writing

This reference covers the grammar, spelling, and vocabulary needed to write natural, correct Haitian Creole for survey questions, field labels, choice lists, and enumerator descriptions. Read this file when building forms in Haitian Creole to ensure the language sounds natural and follows proper conventions.

## Spelling and Accent Rules

Haitian Creole is a phonetic language -- every letter is pronounced, and every pronounced sound is written. This makes spelling consistent once you know the rules.

### Accent Marks

Creole uses the grave accent (`) on three vowels to change their sound:

| Letter | Sound | Example |
|--------|-------|---------|
| è | like "e" in "bed" | lèt (milk), tèt (head), lekòl → lekòl |
| ò | like "aw" in "jaw" | kòb (money), mòn (mountain), lòt (other) |
| à | rare, marks non-nasal "a" | lamàn (manna) |

Without the accent: `e` sounds like "ay" in "say", `o` sounds like "oh" in "go".

**For survey writing:** Always include accent marks in labels and descriptions. They change meaning and pronunciation. `kote` (where) and `kòte` would be different words. Enumerators need correct accents to read questions properly.

### Nasal Sounds

When `a`, `e`, or `o` are followed by `n` at the end of a word or before a consonant, they form nasal sounds (pronounced through the nose, the `n` is not pronounced separately):

| Spelling | Sound | Example |
|----------|-------|---------|
| an | nasalized "ah" | lajan (money), manje (food) |
| en | nasalized "eh" | men (hand), pen (bread) |
| on | nasalized "oh" | pon (bridge), bon (good) |

**Exception:** When followed by a second `n` or `m`, pronounce the `n` separately: `jenn` (young), `fanm` (woman), `manm` (member).

**Non-nasal marking:** When `an`, `en`, `on` should NOT be nasal, a grave accent marks them: `Amerikèn` (American woman), `bòn` (maid).

### Key Consonant Rules

| Letter | Rule | Example |
|--------|------|---------|
| ch | like "sh" in "show" | fache (upset), machin (car) |
| g | always hard like "got" | gason (boy), gen (have) |
| j | like "zh" in "pleasure" | jwe (play), jodi (today) |
| ou | like "oo" in "food" | moun (person), ou (you) |
| r | soft, at back of throat | rive (arrive) |
| s | always "s", never "z" | kasa (cassava) |
| w | like English "w" | wè (see), wi (yes) |
| y | like English "y" in "yes" | youn (one), yo (they) |

## Grammar Essentials

### No Gender

Creole has no masculine/feminine distinction. No agreement needed (unlike French):
- yon bon doktè (a good doctor) -- same whether male or female
- moun malad la (the sick person) -- no gender marker

### Pronouns

| Creole | English | Short form |
|--------|---------|------------|
| Mwen | I / me / my | M (before consonants) |
| Ou | you / your | W (before vowels) |
| Li | he / she / it / his / her | L (before vowels) |
| Nou | we / us / our; also: you (plural) | N |
| Yo | they / them / their | Y |

Pronouns don't change between subject and object: `Mwen wè li` (I see him/her), `Li wè mwen` (He/She sees me).

**Possessives** follow the noun: `kay mwen` (my house), `fanmi ou` (your family), `non li` (his/her name).

### Articles

**Indefinite article:** `yon` (a/an) -- placed BEFORE the noun: `yon kay` (a house), `yon moun` (a person).

**Definite articles** -- placed AFTER the noun (the form depends on the noun's ending sound):

| Noun ending | Article | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| Vowel (a, e, i, o, ou) | a | dlo a (the water), lari a (the street) |
| Consonant | la | kay la (the house), solèy la (the sun) |
| Nasal sound (an, en, on) | an | lajan an (the money), avyon an (the airplane) |
| Pronounced n or m | nan/lan | fanm nan (the woman), machin nan (the car) |

**Plural:** Add `yo` after the noun: `kay yo` (the houses), `timoun yo` (the children). The noun itself does not change.

### Verb System

Verbs do NOT conjugate -- the same form is used for all persons. Tense is marked with particles before the verb:

| Tense | Marker | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Present (habitual) | (none) | Mwen manje (I eat) |
| Present progressive | ap | Mwen ap manje (I am eating) |
| Past | te | Mwen te manje (I ate) |
| Past progressive | t ap | Mwen t ap manje (I was eating) |
| Future | pral / a | Mwen pral manje (I'm going to eat) |
| Conditional | ta | Mwen ta manje (I would eat) |
| Just completed | fèk | Mwen fèk manje (I just ate) |
| Habitual past | te konn | Mwen te konn manje (I used to eat) |

**"To be"** is usually not expressed: `Mwen malad` (I am sick), `Li isi` (He/She is here). When needed for emphasis: `se` at the beginning (`Se vre` = It's true) or `ye` at the end of questions (`Kijan ou ye?` = How are you?).

### Negation

Place `pa` before the verb: `Li pa malad` (He/She is not sick), `Mwen pa konprann` (I don't understand).

### Prepositions

| Creole | English | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| nan | in, at | nan kay la (in the house) |
| sou | on | sou tab la (on the table) |
| anba | under | anba pyebwa a (under the tree) |
| ak / avèk | with | ak fanmi li (with his/her family) |
| pou | for | pou timoun yo (for the children) |
| bò | near | bò rivyè a (near the river) |
| depi | since | depi 2020 (since 2020) |
| jiska | until | jiska demen (until tomorrow) |

## Question Formation

### Yes/No Questions

Two ways to form yes/no questions:

1. **Rising intonation only:** `Ou malad?` (Are you sick?)
2. **Add `Èske` at the beginning:** `Èske ou malad?` (Are you sick?)

For survey questions, prefer the `Èske` form -- it's clearer and more formal, which works better for reading aloud.

### Information Questions

| Question word | English | Example |
|---------------|---------|---------|
| Kisa / Ki sa | What | Kisa ou fè? (What do you do?) |
| Ki | Which / What | Ki laj ou? (What age are you?) |
| Kilès | Who | Kilès ki chèf kay la? (Who is the head of household?) |
| Ki kote / Kote | Where | Ki kote ou rete? (Where do you live?) |
| Kilè / Ki lè | When | Kilè ou te rive? (When did you arrive?) |
| Poukisa | Why | Poukisa ou pa ale lekòl? (Why don't you go to school?) |
| Kijan / Ki jan | How | Kijan ou jwenn dlo? (How do you get water?) |
| Konbyen | How many/much | Konbyen timoun ou genyen? (How many children do you have?) |

### Survey Question Patterns

**Asking about current state:**
- Èske ou gen... (Do you have...)
- Èske gen... nan kay la (Is there... in the house)
- Ki kalite... ou itilize? (What kind of... do you use?)

**Asking about frequency:**
- Konbyen fwa... (How many times...)
- Konbyen fwa pa semèn ou... (How many times per week do you...)

**Asking about duration:**
- Depi konbyen tan... (For how long... / Since when...)
- Konbyen tan sa fè depi... (How long has it been since...)

**Asking about satisfaction/opinion:**
- Èske ou satisfè ak... (Are you satisfied with...)
- Ki sa ou panse de... (What do you think about...)

**Asking about change over time:**
- Èske sitiyasyon... chanje depi... (Has the situation... changed since...)
- Konpare ak ane pase, èske... (Compared to last year, has...)

## Survey Question Patterns

### Consent

```
Èske ou dakò pou patisipe nan sondaj sa a?
(Do you agree to participate in this survey?)

Nou pral poze ou kèk kesyon sou [sijè]. Enfòmasyon ou bay yo ap rete konfidansyèl.
(We are going to ask you some questions about [topic]. The information you provide will remain confidential.)
```

### Household Demographics

```
Konbyen moun ki abite nan kay sa a?
(How many people live in this house?)

Kilès ki chèf kay la?
(Who is the head of the household?)

Ki laj ou genyen?
(How old are you?)

Ki relasyon ou ak chèf kay la?
(What is your relationship to the head of household?)
```

### Education

```
Èske ou te janm ale lekòl?
(Have you ever attended school?)

Ki dènye klas ou te fini?
(What was the last grade you completed?)

Èske timoun yo ap ale lekòl kounye a?
(Are the children currently attending school?)

Poukisa timoun nan pa ale lekòl?
(Why doesn't the child go to school?)
```

### Health

```
Èske ou te malad nan dènye 30 jou yo?
(Have you been sick in the last 30 days?)

Ki kote ou ale lè ou malad?
(Where do you go when you are sick?)

Èske ou te pran vaksen...?
(Have you been vaccinated for...?)

Konbyen fwa ou te vizite yon doktè oswa sant sante nan dènye 12 mwa yo?
(How many times did you visit a doctor or health center in the last 12 months?)
```

### Water & Sanitation

```
Ki sous dlo prensipal fanmi an itilize pou bwè?
(What is the main water source the family uses for drinking?)

Konbyen tan sa pran pou ale chèche dlo, plen, epi retounen?
(How long does it take to go fetch water, fill up, and return?)

Ki kalite twalèt menaj la itilize?
(What type of toilet does the household use?)
```

### Livelihoods

```
Ki aktivite prensipal ou fè pou viv?
(What is your main activity for a living?)

Èske ou gen lòt sous revni?
(Do you have other sources of income?)

Konbyen lajan fanmi an touche pa mwa an mwayèn?
(How much money does the family earn per month on average?)
```

### Closing Questions (Interview/Discussion Style)

```
Èske gen lòt bagay ou ta renmen pataje sou [sijè]?
(Is there anything else you would like to share about [topic]?)

Èske gen lòt bagay ou ta renmen ajoute?
(Is there anything else you would like to add?)

Èske ou gen lòt komantè ou ta renmen pataje?
(Do you have any other comments you'd like to share?)

Anvan nou fini, èske gen lòt bagay sou [sijè] ou panse ki enpòtan pou mansyone?
(Before we finish, is there anything else about [topic] that you think is important to mention?)
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using French spelling instead of Creole:** Write `lekòl` not `école`, `doktè` not `docteur`, `fanm` not `femme`.

2. **Adding gender agreement:** Creole has no gender. Don't add masculine/feminine endings.

3. **Conjugating verbs:** Verbs don't change form. Use tense markers (te, ap, pral) instead.

4. **Putting articles before nouns:** Definite articles go AFTER: `kay la` (the house), not `la kay`.

5. **Forgetting accent marks:** `kote` and `kòte` have different pronunciations. Always include accents in labels and descriptions.

6. **Using "est-ce que" instead of "Èske":** Write `Èske`, not the French form.

7. **Plural "s":** Don't add "s" to pluralize. Use `yo` for definite plural: `kay yo` (the houses).

8. **Using "de" for possession:** Use pronoun after noun: `kay mwen` (my house), not `de mwen kay`.

9. **Missing nasal distinctions:** `an`, `en`, `on` at end of words are nasal sounds. If the preceding vowel needs to be non-nasal, mark it: `Amerikèn`.

10. **Inconsistent accent handling between labels and data:** Labels get full accents (`Agrikiltè`), data names and choice values get accents stripped (`agrikilte`). See the accent marks section in SKILL.md.
